The Lok Sabha election result will come out tomorrow, May 23. The counting of the votes polled in the Lok Sabha Election 2019 will begin at 8am across 542 Lok Sabha constituencies at various counting booths (election in one constituency -- Vellore in Tamil Nadu -- was cancelled.The Lok Sabha election was a tough fight with political parties campaigning for weeks and months to convince voters that they held the best hope for the future of India.On election result date 2019, which is May 23, the fates of PM Narendra Modi and Rahul Gandhi -- the most prominent opposition face right now -- will be revealed.All eyes now are on the Election Commission as the election result time draws closer.The Lok Sabha election result 2019 will be a product of polls conducted in seven phases over 542 constituencies (voting in one constituency -- Tamil Nadu's Vellore was cancelled).The result of election 2019 will decide if PM Modi gets another chance to form the government at the Centre.Lok Sabha election 2019 kicked-off on April 11, the day of voting in Phase 1. The election ended on May 19, four days before the result day.The Election Commission will begin counting the votes polled in this election at 8am on May 23. The Election Commission will provide real-time trends and results on eci.gov.in and results.eci.gov.in.

SPORTS-

India hit the nets at the Oval Cricket Ground a day after landing in London for the 2019 Cricket World Cup. All of India's selected 15 for the World Cup played the Indian Premier League and several played starring roles for their respective teams.England are hosting the World Cup for the 5th time and for the 1st time in 20 years. Most of India's cricketers have plenty of experience playing in England. India had reached the final of the Champions Trophy in England in 2017 and in 2018, India had toured England to play 3 T20Is, 3 ODIs and 5 Tests.India start their World Cup campaign against South Africa on June 5 but before that, they will play 2 practice matches against New Zealand on May 25 and against Bangladesh on May 28.Over the last two years, India have been in good ODI form. In early 2018, India won a historic series in South Africa but went down 1-2 to England before winning the Asia Cup and a home series against West Indies. India started 2019 with ODI series wins in Australia and New Zealand.

As the Indian team works on getting the batting order right for the 2019 World Cup, legendary batsman Sachin Tendulkar feels India's usual No. 5, MS Dhoni, should be batting at the same spot in England.Lately, India's number 4 has become a musical chair affair with a couple of batsmen being tried at the spot. With an endless debate on the best suited player for three down, India vice-captain Rohit Sharma had suggested that MS Dhoni should be moved promoted to play at No. 4."My personal opinion is Dhoni should be batting five. I still don't know what the team combination would be, but if you are going Rohit and Shikhar as openers, to Virat [Kohli] at No. 3 and whoever at No. 4 then Dhoni could be No. 5. Then Hardik Pandya, an explosive player, follows them," Sachin Tendulkar told in an interview to ESPNcricinfo."That way the experienced batsmen are well spaced out and Dhoni can stretch the game towards the end where he himself can be explosive along with Hardik," Tendulkar said.

Prime Minister Theresa May set out on Tuesday a "new deal" for Britain's departure from the European Union, offering sweeteners to opposition parties in her fourth attempt to break an impasse in parliament over Brexit.Three years since Britain voted to leave the EU and almost two months after the planned departure date, May is mounting a last bid to try to get the deeply divided parliament's backing for a divorce deal, to leave the office with some kind of legacy.The odds do not look good. Despite offering what she described as "significant further changes", many lawmakers, hardened in their positions, have already decided not to vote next month for the Withdrawal Agreement Bill, legislation which implements the terms of Britain's departure.Speaking at the headquarters of PricewaterhouseCoopers, May appealed to lawmakers to get behind her deal, offering the prospect of a possible second referendum on the agreement and closer trading arrangements with the EU as incentives."I say with conviction to every MP or every party: I have compromised, now I ask you to compromise," she said......Read More
